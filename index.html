Research projects

  • MoCHi

    MoCHi is a software model checker to verify the correcctness of assertions in given programs. It abstract the execution paths of the program by higher-order recursion schemes and perform the verification over them.

    My contribution is in the predicate abstraction of MoCHi. It uses Craig's interpolation for finding appropriate dependent types that abstract the program paths. The demonstration will be soon available on the web, and the result will be submitted to SAS 2013.

  • Why3

    Why3 is a formal verification platform, that enables users to show the correctness of the given program. Its importance lies in the ability to split the large proving goal into smaller ones by introducing intermediate lemmas, which can be separately treated as sub-goals.

    I involved to the Why3 development project by extending the existing proof library. Some of the added theories are essential for proving the correctness of many discrete algorith on graphs (e.g. pigeonhole principle). Those theories themselves are proved to be correct by Why3, and ready to be reused for proving large algorithms.

    My contribution to Why3 includes the proof of some well-known algorithms. One among them is available here.
